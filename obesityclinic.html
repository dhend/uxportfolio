Visualization of Obesity Clinical Trials

Data Visualization Project

Motivation

  • The obeisty rate increases significantly each year in the U.S.
  • Obesity has become a serious health issue facing the U.S.
  • We're trying to use a visualization tool to display obesity rate and related clinical trial information from 2003 to 2017.

We're trying to answer following questions:

  • What is the distribution of obesity trials in the United States?
    • Which state in the United States has the most clinical trials of obesity and which is the least?
    • Is there any difference in clinical trials of obesity in different states? If there are differences, what are those differences?
  • What are the conditions that are frequently studied together with obesity?
  • What are the interventions used?
  • How did trials characters change over time? Are there any trends shown by the visualization?

Data Collection

  • Obesity rate by state

    • The data on obesity rate by states from 2003 to 2017 are retrieved from CDC.

  • State demographic data

    • State demographic data include median household income, poverty rate, and state population from 2003 to 2017. The date is downloaded from US census bureau.

  • Obesity Clinical Trials data

    • Obtained from clinicaltrials.gov, which is a database of privately and publicly funded clinical studies conducted around the world managed by the U.S. National Library of Medicine.

Design Justification

We designed a choropleth and a multi-series line chart to display obesity rate information. Then, we used individual charts to display clinical trial information.

  • Colorblind friendly

    • We used colorbrewer when choosing the color schema for the choropleth, to make it colorblind friendly.

  • Stability of graph

    • When hovering on the timeline, the choropleth and the clinical trial charts can change smoothly and simultaneously to show the corresponding information in the specific year, making it easier to explore and analyze the data.

  • Appropriate chart type selection

    • Most of the clinical trial information are categorical variables. According to the number of categories in each variable, we selected appropriate charts to display the information, making it easier to read and distinguish between different categories.

Findings

  • The amount of obesity-related clinical trial is increasing as time passing by, as well as the obesity rate. By exploring the choropleth, we found that Florida had the most obesity-related clinical trial among other states.
  • We can also notice that there is a difference in the number of clinical trials in different states. It seems that more clinical trials are on the east side of the country relative to the west side of the country.
  • To observe the Co-studied condition graph, we could see that diabetes has been studied most relevant to obesity in 2017, following by metabolism and nutrition disorder.
  • The intervention used most is behavioral action from 2003 to 2017.
  • For funding source, we found that NIH is the main funding source for all the clinical trial studies except others.

Reflection

  • By interacting with the visualized product, we can answer many questions more efficiently and effectively.
  • Interactive visualization provides researchers and users a more direct way to explore and analyze the big deal of data.
