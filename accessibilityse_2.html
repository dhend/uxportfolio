Disability Disclosure in the Sharing Economy Platforms

User Research

Background


  • What is Sharing Economy?
    • New online platforms which connect individuals who are not familiar with each other to exchange goods and labor, e.g., AirBnb, Uber, Lyft, Rover, etc.
  • Why Sharing Economy is potential for people with disabilities?
    • The work is more flexible on Sharing Economy platforms.
    • People with disabilities encountered discrimination in conventional work environment.
    • It's easier for people with disabilities to find part-time job in relative to people without disabiliti
  • Discrimination in Sharing Economy
    • Gender and racial discrimination against both clients and service providers on sharing economy platforms.

Problems

Whether clients will discriminate service providers with disabilities on sharing economy platforms?

  • Types of disability
    • Visible disabilities: e.g., Wheelchair Users v.s. Non-wheelchair Users
    • Invisible disabilities: Physical Disabilities v.s. Psychiatric Disabilities
  • Types of sharing economy work
    • Mobility requirement: low v.s. high
    • Expertise requirement: low v.s. high
    • Online work v.s. offline work

Method

  • 3 × 2 between-groups experiment
  • 97 participants
  • Independent Variables:
    • Invisible Disability Status:
      • Physical Disability
      • Psychiatric Disability
      • No Disability
    • Platforms:
      • Online Work: Fiverr
      • Offline Work: Rover
  • Dependent Variables:
    • Employment Suitability

Procedures

  1. Participants were getting familiar with the tasks on the platform.
    • Reading description
    • Sample message
    • Platform's official websites
  2. Participants imagine themselves using the service and evaluate a profile a service provider on the platform.
  3. Participants completed surveys evaluating service provider's credibility and employability suitability.

A mockup worker profile on Rover. The service provider discloses her disability status in the second paragraph.

Results

Statistic Results

Means and 95% confidence intervals of Trustworthiness, Attractiveness, Competence, and Employability Suitability by profiles’ Disability Types and SE platforms.

Across platforms, service providers with invisible physical and psychiatric disability got similar scores in Trustworthiness, Competence, and Employability Suitability when working in Fiverr and Rover.

For service providers without disabilities, they got higher scores in all the dependent variables on Rover than in Fiverr.

The results indicated that there was no significant effect of disability status and types of sharing economy platforms on Trustworthiness, Attractiveness, Competence, and Employability Suitability.

Open-end Respnses Analysis

Affinity diagram of analyzing responses about additional info and decision reasons.

Recommendations

  • Considering service providers with disabilities might have less work experience or working history than other service providers, features on the platforms displaying work experience, for example, the number of reviews, years of experiences, etc., might not work for service providers with disabilities.
  • Considering the possibility of giving their service providers chances to disclose their disability status to work on the platform.
  • Consider providing disabled service providers with spaces to request appropriate accommodation for their particular health situations.
