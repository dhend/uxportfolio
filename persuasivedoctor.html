Persuasive Virtual Doctor

Virtual Agent Design Research

Background


  • Each year, patient nonadherence to treatment advice costs the US healthcare system more than $300 billion and results in 250,000 deaths.
  • Developing virtual consultations to promote adherence could improve public health while cutting healthcare costs and usage.
  • Inconsistencies in the realism of computer-animated humans may cause the uncanny valley effect, which could reduce a virtual doctor’s credibility and patients’ adherence.
  • The research will provide an empirical foundation for designing virtual characters.

Theory


How people have been persuaded?

Heuristic and Systematic Model

Facts


  • In conventional clinical scenarios, ...
    • Patients' perceptions of their doctor's warmth and competence increase adherence.
    • Warmth and competence improve the patients' prognosis and quality of life by increasing adherence.
  • In virtual scenarios, ...
    • Virtual humans offer a familar interface that can be used to change people's beliefs, attitudes, intentions, and behaviors.
  • Both real and virtual humans vary in form, behavior, and interactivity, however, virtual humans also vary in human realism.
  • The features that are inconsistent in their realism elicit feelings of eeriness, a phenomenon known as the uncanny valley.

Uncanny Valley graph from Mori.

Question


A fundamental problem with virtual humans:

While delivering intended warmth and competence cues, virtual humans also deilvered unintended cues about inconsistent realism

Inconsistent realism → Uncanny valley → Adherence intention

Methods

  • 2 × 2 × 2 between-groups posttest-only experiment
  • 738 participants
  • Independent Variables:
    • Doctor's Character (good or poor bedside manner)
    • Outcome (received a fellowship or sued for malpractice)
    • Depiction (a recorded video of a real huamn actor or of his 3D animated double)
  • Dependent Variables:
    • Warmth and competence
    • Human realism and eeriness
    • Adherence Intention
    • Enjoyment

The real human doctor and his digital double used in the experiment.

Scenario

The scenario process

The participants were given below instructions:

One afternoon, you visit Dr. Richards, a primary care physician, for a routine physical examination. As instructed by the nurse, you ate nothing on the morning of your appointment.

After the examination, you learn from Dr. Richards that your blood sugar tested higher than normal. You undergo some further blood work and return to the waiting room. After a while, Nurse Larson calls you back into the doctor's office.

Results

Adherence Intention (A) and consultation Enjoyment (B) were significant greater for the doctor with good bedside manner than the doctor with poor bedside manner.

Means and 95% confidence intervals of Adherence Intention and Enjoyment plotted by Character and Depiction.

In the path models, the green arrow indicates a positive direct effect, the red arrow indicates a negative direct effect, and the standardized estimate (β) indicates the strength of the effect. The independent variable Depiction, indicating computer animated or real, is the exogenous variable. In Model 2, 3, and 4, Enjoyment mitigated Eeriness by increasing Human Realism. All models had good local fit, but only Model 2 and 4 had good global fit. Model 4 is the final model.

Path models of the indirect effect of Enjoyment of computer animation on Eeriness.
